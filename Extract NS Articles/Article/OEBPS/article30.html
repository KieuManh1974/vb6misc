An extra dimension for aerial images

Google Earth has a new competitor when it comes to producing aerial 3D images. A system called Pix4D, produced by a spin-off from the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology in Lausanne, can take 2D aerial photos and render them in 3D in about 30 minutes. Images of the desired area captured by a cheap flying drone are uploaded to a Pix4D server, where software detects points common to several shots. This allows it to stitch them together to form a 3D image. Photos taken at different times can be layered to show how the landscape is changing.

Stealth helicopter's secret is out

They might have got their man, but US forces appear to have left behind the remains of a hitherto secret stealth helicopter after their raid on Osama bin Laden's compound in Abbottabad, Pakistan. The troops attempted to destroy the evidence by setting fire to the downed machine, but large chunks survived. What was left suggests it was a modified Black Hawk designed for stealth. The engine shroud has angled edges that may make it less visible to radar, and a dish-shaped cover over the tail rotor looks as if it should quell noise. People in the area reportedly did not hear the helicopters until they were overhead.

That's the situation in which the phrase "that's what she said" is used to highlight a hidden or possibly unintended lewd meaning; Brits may say "as the actress said to the bishop" instead. Their Deviant (Double Entendre via Noun Transfer) system rates words and sentences for their double entendre potential. Nouns it has learned to look out for include "rod" and "meat", while mundane adjectives with a potentially raunchy meaning include "hot" and "wet".
